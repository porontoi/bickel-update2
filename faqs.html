ACA/Obamacare is major medical coverage. These plans are best for those with a lengthy history of pre-existing conditions. They are only affordable IF you qualify for a government subsidy on the monthly premiums. These subsidies are based on your income AND place you in a pool of covered individuals with higher yearly claims – whose cost fall back to all policy holders upon renewal.

If your income level precludes you from that discount, the plans offered through ACA are expensive and have limited benefits. Additionally, the cost continues to increase with each added dependent. You are not rewarded for your health status but penalized for your hard-earned income

Employer plans are another viable option for the employee. However, additional dependents coverage drives up the premium cost significantly. These plans also involve being in a pool of covered individuals who have costly, pre-existing conditions. The premium you will pay is based on the cost to the company and is spread out evenly across all participants in the plan – meaning you pay a higher premium because of the health of other participants. Moreover, the plans cease if employment ends.

Private insurance is based on YOUR health. The pool of covered individuals is in good to excellent health. Therefore, the premiums are significantly lower, and they offer better coverage.

There are no contractual commitments with a private plan, and you are free to cancel or change your coverage at any time.
